Elder Care will call your parents thrice a week to check on their well being. Pre-prepared questions which cover common medical and independent living matters will be asked. If they require handling, will be attended to immediately.

Our trained Elder Care Officers (ECOs) who undergo constant training and most are ex-army personnel will visit them once a week.

On the day of their visit they will assist in whatever help is required by the Elder.

This could include chaperoning them for a medical check-up. Thereafter Elder Care International will be responsible for sourcing the report, updating your Elders’s record in our systems and informing you about the report.

During their visits to your home, ECOs will also capture basic health related readings like BP, Sugar level and body temperature. These will be updated in their records to which you will have full access.

Medical emergencies can spring up anytime and anywhere. Whatever time and wherever they arise, ECOs will reach your Parent/Elder in the shortest possible time and ensure that your Parent/Elder reaches the best* medical care.

You may be 24 hours or more away, with no access to a relative or friend. Elder Care will be there* to be at your Elder/Parent’s side until the time the registered local guardian or you arrive.

Every step of the way every move of the ECO is time-stamped and thus automatically monitoring the time the ECO reached the Elder, the time they reach a hospital and the time the Elder is admitted to the hospital.

*ECOs will stay until local guardian or the next of kin arrive. Instances where the time exceeds 2 hours. Additional attendant-time charges will apply on 3 hourly basis.

You, living abroad, will be informed of the care being given on every step of the way.
